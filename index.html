Tooth Care Tips

  • Brush your teeth at least twice a day with fluoride toothpaste for two minutes each time.
  • Floss daily to remove plaque and food particles between teeth that a toothbrush can't reach.
  • Use mouthwash to help reduce plaque and prevent gum disease.
  • Eat a balanced diet low in sugary foods and drinks to prevent cavities.
  • Visit your dentist regularly for check-ups and cleanings, ideally every six months.
  • Replace your toothbrush every three to four months or when bristles are frayed.
  • Drink plenty of water to help wash away food particles and keep your mouth hydrated.
  • Avoid smoking and tobacco products, as they can cause gum disease and oral cancer.
  • Wear a mouthguard if you play contact sports to protect your teeth from injury.
  • Teach children good oral hygiene habits early to prevent future dental problems.
  • Use a soft-bristled toothbrush to avoid damaging gums and enamel.
  • Limit acidic foods and drinks, as they can erode tooth enamel over time.
  • Rinse your mouth with water after eating if you can't brush immediately.
  • Consider using dental sealants for children to protect molars from decay.
  • If you grind your teeth at night, talk to your dentist about a custom mouthguard.
